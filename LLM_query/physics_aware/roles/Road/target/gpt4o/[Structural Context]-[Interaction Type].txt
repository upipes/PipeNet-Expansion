1. cavity: In the road subsurface context, cavities are interaction points where radar waves encounter irregular air-filled spaces leading to subsidence. The structural context reveals strong bright hyperbolic reflections in GPR B-scans due to cavities' distinct dielectric contrast with surrounding soil or concrete. Commonly observed in unpaved and poorly maintained asphalt areas, cavities show multiple reflections and diffraction patterns, indicating their presence and shape. The interaction suggests increased wave travel time and dispersal, making their detection more certain.

2. crack: Cracks within the subsurface of road structures appear as sudden horizontal discontinuities on GPR B-scans, particularly over concrete and asphalt surfaces. Structures show pronounced reflections at the crack interfaces where waves meet air, causing phase shifts. Typically, the waveform exhibits abrupt breaks or changes near the surface, signaling the presence of structural failure points. Interaction-type highlights near-surface interactions causing higher reflection intensity; however, due to complex geometry, prediction of continuity can be uncertain, especially if cracks are narrow or not consistently aligned.

3. loose: Loose areas in roadbeds primarily interact within unpaved and occasionally asphalt environments, complicating structural context with diffuse and chaotic reflections. Such areas manifest as regions with smeared waveforms due to lower density and porosity causing partial absorption and scattering of waves. Interaction appears as irregular widespread reflections where increased wave path length and incoherent wave behavior indicate loose materials. There is inherent uncertainty due to the high environmental variability in looseness across different roadside conditions.

4. normal: Normal subsurface conditions show consistent and undisturbed wave propagation patterns in B-scans, typical of unaltered road substrata. Structurally, these environments present smooth, even layering with clear stratification undisturbed by subsurface bodies. Interaction is marked by uniform signal stability and regularity in waveforms, implying absence of subsurface anomalies. Certainty of normalcy is high due to systematic wave transmission through solid and homogenous dielectric media across concrete, asphalt, and well-maintained unpaved roads.

5. pipeline: Pipelines exhibit specific interaction signatures across various road types, commonly generating clear hyperbolic reflections due to their consistent geometric profile and metallic characteristic enhancing wave reflection. In the structural context, pipelines under asphalt and concrete appear as linear, high-contrast anomalies frequently surrounded by symmetric layering, while in loose unpaved areas they might be accompanied by less orderly reflections. Detection certainty is increased by pipeline reflectivity and consistent geometric cues in B-scan images.
