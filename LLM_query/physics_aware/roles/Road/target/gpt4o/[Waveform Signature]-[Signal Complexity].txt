1. cavity: The cavity exhibits a hyperbolic waveform signature with strong reflected EM waves due to the significant dielectric contrast between air and surrounding materials. This results in high signal amplitude and pronounced diffraction patterns. Signal complexity increases with irregular cavity shapes, causing secondary reflections or scattered signals. The hyperbolic apex often aligns with the cavity's center, though irregular boundaries can blur this cue.

2. crack: Cracks manifest as abrupt discontinuities in otherwise uniform waveforms, with significant reflection peaks at the air-filled gap interfaces. Minimal diffraction occurs due to their thin geometry. Signal complexity is moderate, influenced by the crack's inclination and connectivity, often with small shifts in phase or amplitude at crack edges. Horizontal cracks predominantly appear as parallel high-strength reflectors in the B-scan.

3. loose: Loose soil generates diffuse and chaotic waveform signatures due to inconsistent material density and porosity. Reflected signals are weaker and scattered across the B-scan, lacking distinct hyperbolic or linear patterns. Signal complexity is high as the irregular material distribution alters the coherence of wave propagation, leading to fragmented or noisy reflections with overlapping layers.

4. normal: The normal subsurface state is characterized by continuous, smooth waveforms with predictable attenuation and low scattering. Signal reflections follow uniform patterns governed by steady material transitions and layers. Complexity is minimal, as wave propagation faces no significant interruption. Waveform signatures often show clear horizontal stratification corresponding to layered underground structures.

5. pipeline: A pipeline produces a distinct hyperbolic waveform consistent with the high dielectric constant of the pipeline material relative to the surrounding environment. The hyperbola's width and symmetry depend on the pipeline's depth and curvature. Signal complexity is moderate, with weaker secondary reflections unless adjacent to other anomalies. The hyperbolic apex strongly indicates the pipeline's position.
