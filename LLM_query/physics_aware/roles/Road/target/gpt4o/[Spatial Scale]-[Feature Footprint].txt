1. cavity: In the "[Spatial Scale]-[Feature Footprint]" view, cavities manifest as distinct hyperbolic reflections with strong amplitude due to the contrast between the air space and surrounding materials. These structures often appear irregular in shape and can cause echoes or potential multiple reflections because air has a significantly lower dielectric constant than typical subsurface materials. The geometric cue is a void-like gap that could cause localized subsidence on the road surface.

2. crack: Cracks appear as linear, discontinuous patterns across the GPR B-scan, showing up as strong, sharp reflections due to the air-filled horizontal gaps within the compact structure of the road. These reflections disrupt the continuity of subsurface strata, creating abrupt amplitude changes. Cracks are typically narrow in footprint but can extend horizontally, indicating potential pathways for water infiltration or further degradation.

3. loose: Loose materials are depicted as areas with diffuse and chaotic reflection patterns, lacking clear geometric shapes, due to their high porosity and irregularity. These reflections are less coherent as compared to more structured features, indicative of soil and air mixtures leading to lower cohesion. The waveforms might appear scattered, lacking the organized hyperbolas or linearity of denser materials.

4. normal: In this view, normal sections of the road present as continuous, uniform layers with consistent amplitude and regular waveforms that follow expected geo-stratigraphic lines without interruption or anomaly. These areas display clear and repetitive patterns consistent with homogeneous material properties and stable subsurface conditions.

5. pipeline: Pipeline presence is indicated by consistent, repeating hyperbolic reflections that exhibit both symmetrical and periodic attributes, coupled with strong and distinct echoes due to the high contrast between the pipeline material and surrounding soil. The geometric signature typically forms consistent parabolas for each occurrence, suggesting a linear trajectory consistent with known pipeline installations.
