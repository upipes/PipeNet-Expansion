3. **loose**: [Structural Context-Interaction Type] Loose soil regions cause irregular wave propagation due to chaotic electromagnetic scattering and diffusive reflections from porous and inhomogeneous material distributed over neighboring compact layers; structural interaction indicators here include variable density profiles and unpredictable interfaces.

4. **normal**: [Structural Context-Interaction Type] Normal areas maintain homogeneous material structure and consistent layer binding, resulting in smooth wave propagation with minimal scattering or reflection variability; weak interaction evidence reinforces undisturbed subsurface conditions.

5. **pipeline**: [Structural Context-Interaction Type] Metallic or non-metallic pipelines interact strongly with their containing medium by generating focused reflections and potential hyperbolic diffraction patterns, as their geometric and material properties create predictable waveform artifacts with respect to compact underground layers.
